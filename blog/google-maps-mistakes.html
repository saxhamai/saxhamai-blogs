Common Google Maps Mistakes That Hurt Local Businesses

Published on Jan 02, 2026 ‚Ä¢ By Nitin Pawar

I recently tried to visit a computer repair shop in Navi Mumbai. Google Maps said "Open." When I reached there at 8 PM, the shutter was down.

Result? I went to their competitor. That business didn't just lose one sale; they lost a lifetime customer.

For local businesses in India, your Google Maps profile is more important than your Instagram page. Here are the 3 silent mistakes killing your daily leads.

1. The "Open 24 Hours" Lie

Many business owners set their hours to "24 Hours" thinking it looks impressive. Don't do this. Google verifies location data. If customers report you as closed, Google trusts the user, not you. Your ranking drops immediately.

Fix: Set accurate hours. If you take a lunch break from 2-3 PM, mention it. Accuracy builds trust.

2. Using Stock Photos or Logos Only

Customers want to know if your place is real. A profile with only a logo looks fake. A profile with stock photos of "happy Americans in suits" looks suspicious.

  • Good: A photo of your shop front (signboard visible).
  • Good: A photo of your team working.
  • Bad: blurry WhatsApp images or generic internet photos.

3. Ignoring Negative Reviews

You cannot delete bad reviews. But ignoring them is worse. When you reply calmly to a bad review, future customers see that you care. Silence looks like arrogance.
