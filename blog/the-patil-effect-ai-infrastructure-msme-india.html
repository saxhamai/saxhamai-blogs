The “Patil Effect”: How Anthropic’s AI Infrastructure Shift Is Reshaping IT — And Why Indian MSMEs Should Pay Attention

Published on Feb 17, 2026 • By Nitin Pawar • 7 min read

In early 2026, global IT markets reacted sharply to a shockwave few expected.

Major IT service company valuations saw sudden drops as analysts began using a new phrase: “The Patil Effect.”

Named after Anthropic CTO Rahul Patil, this term refers to a deeper structural shift: AI infrastructure becoming so efficient and cheap that entire enterprise workflows can now be automated by always-on AI agents. Not just chatbots. Not just copilots. But full workflow automation.

For the global IT services industry, this raises serious existential questions. For Indian MSMEs, however, it may be the biggest opportunity in decades.

In this article:

  • What Exactly Is the “Patil Effect”?
  • Why This Matters for the Global IT Sector
  • The Twist: A Massive Opportunity for Indian MSMEs
  • What MSMEs Can Do Today (Real Use Cases)
  • Practical Strategy for the Next 5 Years

What Exactly Is the “Patil Effect”?

At its core, the Patil Effect is about infrastructure efficiency driving radical cost reduction.

Anthropic’s engineering optimizations around the Claude model have reportedly drastically reduced the cost and latency of running advanced AI systems at scale. Through highly efficient GPU utilization, speculative decoding, and faster response pipelines, they have unlocked an "always-on agent architecture."

The result? Enterprise-level AI workflows that were once expensive and slow are now cheap and fast enough for everyday business use.

What previously required large teams, multi-year contracts, and expensive SaaS layers can now be done by AI agents running continuously in the background.

Why This Matters for the Global IT Sector

Traditional IT services follow a predictable model: Large transformation projects with heavy manpower, multi-year timelines, and recurring maintenance contracts. AI agents challenge this structure fundamentally.

If an AI system can read legacy code, handle compliance checks, review contracts, and even write and test software autonomously, the dependency on large outsourced teams reduces significantly.

The Real Disruption: Speed + Cost

  • Earlier: ERP implementation cost crores and took 6–24 months.
  • Now: AI-assisted implementation costs lakhs and takes weeks.
  • Earlier: Compliance reporting required a dedicated team.
  • Now: It's an automated AI workflow.

The fear for large IT firms isn’t immediate collapse; it is long-term margin compression. Investors reacted early because they see that AI agents can replace repetitive IT work and shorten project cycles.

The Twist: This Is Huge for Indian MSMEs

While large IT firms worry about revenue compression, Indian MSMEs gain an asymmetric advantage.

Why? Because MSMEs never had access to large consulting firms or expensive enterprise automation tools in the first place. AI now democratizes that access.

The biggest advantage is the collapse of cost. Custom software or complex setups that used to cost ₹10–50 lakh are now accessible via AI-assisted tools and low-cost APIs. This changes the power structure, allowing small businesses to deploy automation previously reserved for giants.

What MSMEs Can Do Today (Real Use Cases)

Indian businesses are uniquely positioned due to high developer density, cost sensitivity, and an aggressive scaling mindset. Here is how MSMEs can apply this today:

  • 1. Internal Automation: Deploy AI agents to generate invoices, manage inventory, track payments, and draft routine emails.
  • 2. Compliance Workflows: Automate GST summaries, vendor checks, and document processing.
  • 3. Sales Support: Use AI to respond to leads 24/7, qualify prospects, and create initial proposals.
  • 4. IT Operations (For small service providers): Automate remote diagnostics, ticket classification, log analysis, and AMC reporting.

Practical Strategy for Indian MSMEs

The Patil Effect doesn’t mean IT jobs disappear; it means IT services compress, and new roles emerge. The winning model will be AI for execution, Humans for judgment.

Strategic Steps for the Next 5 Years:

  1. Start using AI internally: Don’t wait for clients to ask. Use AI to automate your own reports and operations first to reduce internal costs.
  2. Shift your service offering: Instead of offering "Manual IT support," start offering "AI-enabled IT support."
  3. Build small, niche tools: Create specific inventory agents, service dashboards, or reporting bots tailored to your industry.
  4. Upskill teams aggressively: Train engineers not just on coding, but on prompt engineering, API usage, and automation design.

Final Thought

Every major technology shift creates fear first, and opportunity later. The Patil Effect is not just about AI infrastructure details; it’s about who adapts fastest.

Large companies move slowly. MSMEs move fast. This may be the first time small Indian businesses get access to enterprise-grade intelligence at near-startup cost. As we discussed in our analysis of Budget 2026, the environment is ripe for growth. Those who adopt early won’t just survive—they’ll leap ahead.
